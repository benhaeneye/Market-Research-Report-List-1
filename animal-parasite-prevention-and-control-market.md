<p><p>The animal parasite prevention and control market comprises three primary types: topical, injection, and oral medications. Topical medications are applied directly to the skin, effectively controlling external parasites like fleas and ticks. Injection medications offer a long-lasting solution by delivering active ingredients through a single injection, targeting both internal and external parasites. Oral medications are ingested, providing systemic protection against various internal parasites, such as worms. Each method serves distinct needs, allowing for tailored parasite management in animals.</p></p>
