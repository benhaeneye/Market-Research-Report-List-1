<p><p>The flash memory market is primarily divided into two types: NOR Flash and NAND Flash. NOR Flash offers faster random access and is ideal for code storage, commonly used in embedded systems and mobile devices. In contrast, NAND Flash is optimized for high-density storage, providing slower access times but higher data retention and endurance, making it suitable for applications like USB drives, SSDs, and memory cards. Both types play crucial roles in modern electronics, catering to different storage needs.</p></p>
