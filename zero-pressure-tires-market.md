<p><p>Zero-pressure tires, also known as run-flat tires, allow vehicles to continue operating safely after a puncture or loss of air pressure. The market is divided into two main types: **Self-supporting Type** and **Support Ring Type**. Self-supporting tires have reinforced sidewalls that bear the vehicle's weight independently when deflated. Support Ring Type tires rely on an internal ring or structure to support the vehicle during air loss. Both types enhance safety and convenience, eliminating the need for immediate tire changes after a puncture.</p></p>
